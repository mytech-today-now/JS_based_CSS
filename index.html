Early Vernacular Web (1991–1995)

Vernacular / Geocities Style
Amateur pages built with tiled backgrounds, animated GIFs, “Under Construction” signs & visitor counters.

Table-Based & Frame-Based Layouts (1993–1998)

Table-Based Design
Using HTML tables to approximate multi-column layouts before CSS adoption.
Framesets
Splitting the window into independent panes—navigation, content, etc.

Flash-Based Design (1996–2008)

Flash Animations & Intros
Rich animations and full-screen intros with Macromedia Flash.

Web 2.0 Glossy & Skeuomorphic (2004–2013)

Web 2.0
Glossy buttons, drop shadows, reflections and “beta” tags.
Skeuomorphic Design
Real-world textures (leather, paper, metal) in UI.

Swiss & Minimalist (2000s–present)

Swiss Style
Grid-based layouts, strong typography, generous whitespace.
Minimalist Web Design
Essential-only interfaces with whitespace & high-contrast type.

Responsive & Mobile-First (2010–present)

Responsive Web Design
Fluid grids, flexible images & CSS media queries.
Mobile-First Approach
Design for small screens first, then scale up.

Flat Design & Variations (2010–present)

Flat Design
Simple elements, flat colors, no ornamental textures.
Semi-Flat
Flat plus subtle shadows or minimal gradients.

Parallax & SPAs (2012–2016)

Parallax Scrolling
Layered backgrounds move at different speeds for depth.
Single-Page Apps
JavaScript-driven sites dynamically loading one page.

Progressive Web Apps (2015–present)

Progressive Web Design
App-like experiences with service workers & offline support.

Cinematic & Video Backgrounds (2010s)

Cinematic Design
Full-screen video headers & story-driven layouts.

Brutalism (2015–present)

Brutalist Web Design
Stark pages with raw HTML, plain fonts & bare-bones layouts.
Neo-Brutalism
Brutalist structure plus neon or saturated colors.

Dark Mode (2020–present)

Dark Mode
Light-on-dark color schemes for reduced eye strain.

Neumorphism & Glassmorphism (2020)

Neumorphism
Soft shadows for extruded/inset UI elements.
Glassmorphism
Translucent panels with backdrop blur.

Maximalism & Corporate Memphis (2018–present)

Corporate Memphis
Flat geometric illustrations with bright colors.

Retro & Y2K Aesthetics (2020s)

Retro Web Design
Pixel art, marquee text & neon 90s/00s motifs.
Y2K Web Design
Chrome buttons, metallic gradients & playful type.

3D & Immersive Web (2015–present)

WebGL & 3D
Interactive 3D graphics & VR/AR in-browser.

Inclusive & Accessible Design (Ongoing)

Inclusive Design
ARIA roles, semantic HTML & user-centric accessibility.
